# Research

**Status:** Policy (Layer 1). Read by `.claude/skills/signal-research-dossier/SKILL.md`.
**Owner:** Outbound Signal OS.

## Purpose

Go beneath the first visible signal. A job posting is the tip of the iceberg, and research checks what's underneath it: is the company actually growing, is there real budget pressure, is the signal corroborated by anything else, and is there a fair, specific, evidence-backed angle for outreach.

## Minimum evidence standard

A lead does not proceed to `outreach.md` on the strength of one signal alone. Research must find at least one corroborating data point beyond the original signal before a dossier is considered complete: a second job posting, a funding mention, a leadership change, a news item. One uncorroborated signal produces a thin, generic outreach angle; two related pieces of evidence produce a real "why now."

## Dossier contents

Every dossier records:

- **Signal summary**: what was found and where.
- **Why-now angle**: the specific, evidence-backed reason this company, this week.
- **Proof URLs / source links**: every claim traces to a real source.
- **Safe claims vs. avoid claims**: what the evidence supports saying directly, and what it only implies (don't say it directly).
- **Route recommendation**: which channel and tone fit this lead (feeds `outreach.md`).

## Anti-fabrication rule

Do not fabricate facts, angles, or connections that aren't verified or that feel far-fetched, even if they would make a stronger pitch. A dossier with two well-supported angles beats one with five vague, unverifiable ones. If research comes back thin, the dossier says so honestly rather than padding it: a thin dossier is a signal to hold the lead, not a reason to invent supporting detail.

## What this directive does not cover

How much research is allowed to cost per lead (`cost_control.md`) or how the dossier gets turned into outreach copy (`outreach.md`).
